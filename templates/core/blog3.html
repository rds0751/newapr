What are `Digital Signatures’ and How do they work?

We all hear about digital signatures and how it is changing the system of working. Let us today dive in the world of digital signatures and take a look on how digital signatures are changing the way of working and what benefits it brings to us. To understand why more and more people are being attracted towards digital signatures, let us understand about digital signatures from the very core.

What is a Digital Signature?

Each day, we are transferring a step beforehand in the direction of new technology. The digital documents are also now of upmost significance and need to be authenticated as properly. Much like our files are secured with a signature, our e-documents or digital documents are secured with digital signatures. Digital signature is an idea that has come to apply currently. Digital signature is a kind of electronic signature this is used to encrypt the documents and facilitates to authenticate them. Digital signature is a cryptographic fee that is calculated from the data and a mystery key recognized only by using the signer. They are based on asymmetric-key cryptography where encryption is done with private key and decryption is completed with public key. It is used for Authentication and Non-repudiation. While a file has a digital signature, it ensures the authenticity of a report and additionally uses encryption techniques to provide proof of unique and unmodified file. Digital signatures are used to sign variety of documents such as an email, online order or a watermarked photograph etc. Creating Digital Signature is a very complex process and can be carried out with a computer only. How digital signature works? To understand how digital signature works, let us consider an example: There is an e-commerce company from where you have purchased clothes. It has sent you a bill online and they have signed it with the digital signature. Digital Signature The first step will be generation of keys, the company or server will generate a private key which it will keep for itself and the other key known as public key will be sent to the user. The company will encrypt the bill with the help of its key and user will decrypt it with the help of public key! This ensures that the bill comes from authentic company. Isn’t this method a lot safer than manually signed documents! Let us summarize the working: 1. When the option of signing the document is selected, through various mathematical algorithms a unique fingerprint (or hash) of the document is created. And if there is are changes made, the hash will change and we will be alerted. 2. We encrypt the hash using the signer’s private key. The hash which is encrypted and the signer’s public key are combined into a digital signature, which is appended to the document. 3. Now, we can use the document for distribution. Quite amazing, isn’t it?

The Certificate Authority (CA) are trusted bodies that can issue digital certificates. Users send request to CA for digital certificate. After the CA verifies the information of the user ,it send certificate to the user keeping a copy for itself.
